(Yicai Global) Oct. 21 -- A seven-meter tall smog free tower developed by a Dutch designer is on display in Beijing before beginning a nation-wide tour to promote awareness of air quality and to remind people of their public duty to fight against smog.

A smog free tower developed by Dutch designer Mr. Daan Roosegaarde is being tested in the 798 Art District, an exhibition center of Chinese culture and art, before making its public debut, according to the China Forum of Environmental Journalists.

\r\n\r\n

\r\n\r\n

© VCG

\r\n\r\n

Once properly configured, the tower will go on tour across China as part of a ‘Fight Smog’ campaign.

\r\n\r\n

\r\n\r\n

© VCG

\r\n\r\n

The seven-meter-tall tower is the largest air purifier in the world and generates clean fresh air for people living in the city. Using patented ozone-free ion technology, it purges 30,000 cubic meters of air an hour by capturing 75 percent of all PM2.5 and PM10 fine haze particles in the air. The tower sends fresh air out at all angles creating a loop of fresh air surrounding it.

\r\n\r\n

The ‘Fight Smog’ campaign involves a series of public education events promoting an eco-friendly lifestyle. The tour of the smog free tower is an important part of the campaign. The tower is mainly intended as a warning symbol, to remind the public of their responsibility to fight against smog.
